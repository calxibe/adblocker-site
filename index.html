What it does

Everything you need,
nothing you don't.

Network Blocking

Blocks ads, scripts, and tracking domains at the network level using the Declarative Net Request API. Fast, efficient, and battery-friendly.

Cosmetic Filtering

Hides ad containers, banners, and sponsored content with CSS selector-based filtering. Add your own selectors and choose whether blocked containers collapse completely or stay in the layout.

Cookie Consent Hiding

Dismisses cookie banners, consent modals, and page-locking overlays automatically, then tries to restore page scrolling when a consent layer locks the page.

Tracking Protection

Blocks analytics and tracking scripts from Google Analytics, Google Tag Manager, Microsoft Clarity, comScore, and Bing telemetry.

Notification Blocking

Blocks annoying site notification permission requests before they interrupt you. No more pop-ups asking to send you alerts.

Video Ad Blocking

Blocks pre-roll video ads by intercepting the Google IMA SDK on supported sites. It is currently marked beta and supports extra custom video ad domains from settings.

Per-Site Control

Pause blocking on the current site from the popup, then manage exact hostnames or wildcard patterns from settings when you need broader allowlisting.

Auto-Updating Rules

Filter lists sync silently in the background from a secure API, and the settings page shows the last sync time with a manual refresh button when you want to update immediately.

Settings & Stats

The popup shows how many ads were removed on the current page, while settings give you separate toggles for tracking, notifications, cookie banners, video ads, and custom rule lists.

Add to Your Browser

Confirm

Click Add extension in the confirmation dialog. That's it — blocking starts immediately.

Pin It

Click the extensions icon in your browser's toolbar and pin FQ-AdBlocker for quick access.

4

Customize

Open the settings page to manage wildcard allowlists, custom selectors, custom block domains, custom video ad domains, and rule refresh.
