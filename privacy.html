Privacy Policy

Last Updated: March 30, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how FixGuard ("we", "us", or "our") handles your information when you use our browser extension.

Summary: We do not collect, sell, or share personal data. Blocking happens locally in your browser, and the extension only contacts our rules API at fixguard.org to download updated blocking rules. Those requests are not used for analytics, do not include your browsing history or page content, and fixguard.org does not keep IP logs.

1. Data Collection

FixGuard does not collect any personal data.

The extension reads the content of web pages you visit solely to apply cosmetic filtering — hiding ad containers, cookie consent banners, and other unwanted elements. This processing is performed entirely within your browser on your device.

Network blocking is handled by Chrome's Declarative Net Request API using extension rules. The extension does not intercept request bodies, inspect account data, or transmit your browsing history.

To keep blocking rules current, the extension periodically downloads updated rule data from https://fixguard.org/api/public/adblocker-rules. These requests are limited to rule updates, are not tied to your browsing activity, and fixguard.org does not keep IP logs.

  • We do not track your browsing history.
  • We do not collect analytics or telemetry.
  • We do not send page content, custom rules, or personal identifiers to external servers.
  • We do not sell or share personal data.
  • We do not use cookies.

2. Local Storage

We use your browser's extension storage (chrome.storage.sync and chrome.storage.local) to store extension data on your device or browser profile, such as:

  • Whether blocking is globally enabled or disabled
  • Hide-containers display preference
  • Cookie consent, tracking, notification, and video ad blocking toggles
  • Allowlisted domains and wildcard patterns
  • Custom CSS selectors, block domains, and video ad domains
  • Cached blocking rules downloaded from our rules API
  • Rule sync status and retry timing
  • Local daily counts of blocked ads and cookie overlays

This data remains in your browser storage. If you use Chrome Sync, some settings may sync through your browser profile. We do not receive access to that stored data.

3. Permissions

FixGuard requests the following browser permissions:

  • declarativeNetRequest / declarativeNetRequestWithHostAccess: Used to block ads and tracking requests and apply dynamic allowlist and custom blocking rules.
  • declarativeNetRequestFeedback: Used to query matched rule counts for the popup and debugging. No request payloads are stored or transmitted.
  • storage: Used to persist your settings, cached rules, sync metadata, and local daily stats in browser storage.
  • tabs: Used to detect the current site for per-site allowlisting in the popup.
  • alarms: Used to schedule periodic rule-update checks and retry downloads if the initial sync fails.
  • host_permissions (<all_urls>): Required for the content script to apply cosmetic filtering on all websites.

No permission is used to collect or sell personal information. The only external requests made by the extension are for downloading updated blocking rules.

4. Third-Party Services

FixGuard does not load third-party analytics, ad scripts, or CDN resources into the extension. All extension code runs locally from the extension bundle.

The extension does contact https://fixguard.org/api/public/adblocker-rules to download updated blocking rules. These requests are used only to deliver rule updates, are not used for analytics, and fixguard.org does not keep IP logs.

5. Website

The FixGuard website is a static site hosted on GitHub Pages. It does not use cookies, analytics, or tracking scripts.

6. Your Rights

Since FixGuard does not collect or store personal data, there is no personal data to access, modify, or delete. If you have questions, you may contact us using the information below.

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated date.
